## 2. Grant of access

2.1 **Subscription license.** Subject to this Agreement and payment of applicable fees, Vendor grants Customer a non-exclusive, non-transferable, non-sublicensable right to access and use the Services during the Subscription Term for Customer's internal business purposes.

2.2 **Scope.** The Services are provided as a hosted SaaS platform. Customer does not receive a copy of the software. Access is limited to the tier, seat count, and workspace count specified in the Order Form.

2.3 **Restrictions.** Customer shall not: (a) sublicense, resell, or distribute access to the Services; (b) reverse-engineer, decompile, or attempt to extract source code from the Services; (c) use the Services to build a competing product; (d) circumvent usage limits, authentication, or tenant isolation controls; or (e) transmit malware, perform unauthorized security testing, or interfere with other tenants' use of the Services.

---

## 3. Customer obligations

3.1 **Account security.** Customer is responsible for maintaining the confidentiality of its authentication credentials and for all activity under its account. Customer shall promptly notify Vendor of any unauthorized access.

3.2 **Authorized use.** Customer shall ensure that Authorized Users comply with this Agreement and applicable law. Customer is responsible for the accuracy and legality of Customer Data submitted to the Services.

3.3 **Compliance.** Customer shall comply with all applicable laws, regulations, and industry standards in its use of the Services, including export control and data protection laws applicable to Customer Data.

---

## 4. Fees and payment

4.1 **Fees.** Customer shall pay the fees specified in each Order Form. Current tier definitions, seat prices, platform fees, and run overage rates are documented in [PRICING_PHILOSOPHY.md](PRICING_PHILOSOPHY.md). No prices are hardcoded in this MSA; the Order Form controls.

4.2 **Invoicing and payment terms.** Per the Order Form. Default: Vendor invoices at the start of each billing period; payment is due **Net 30** from invoice date. Run overage (if applicable) is invoiced monthly in arrears.

4.3 **Annual prepay.** If Customer selects annual billing, a discount of two months free applies per [PRICING_PHILOSOPHY.md](PRICING_PHILOSOPHY.md) §5. Annual prepay terms are documented in the Order Form.

4.4 **Taxes.** All fees are exclusive of applicable taxes. Customer is responsible for all taxes, duties, and levies arising from this Agreement, except for taxes based on Vendor's net income.

4.5 **Late payment.** Vendor may suspend access after **15 days** past due with **10 days'** prior written notice, consistent with the Order Form §7.

4.6 **Fee changes.** Vendor may adjust list prices upon **60 days'** written notice. Price changes do not affect the current Subscription Term or one renewal period for existing customers, per [PRICING_PHILOSOPHY.md](PRICING_PHILOSOPHY.md) §5.3.

## 5. Term and termination

5.1 **Term.** This MSA is effective from the date of the first Order Form and continues until all Order Forms have expired or been terminated.

5.2 **Subscription Term.** Each Order Form specifies its own Subscription Term (monthly or annual). Unless either party provides **30 days'** written notice before the end of the current term, the Subscription Term auto-renews for successive periods of the same length at the then-current list price (subject to §4.6).

5.3 **Termination for convenience.** Either party may terminate an Order Form by providing **30 days'** written notice before the end of the current Subscription Term. Termination takes effect at the end of the paid period.

5.4 **Termination for cause.** Either party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other party: (a) materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure within **30 days** of written notice; or (b) becomes subject to insolvency proceedings.

5.5 **Effect of termination.** Upon termination: (a) Customer's access to the Services ceases at the end of the paid period; (b) Vendor-initiated termination entitles Customer to a pro-rata refund of prepaid, unused fees; (c) Customer-initiated early cancellation does not entitle Customer to a refund unless agreed in writing; (d) data handling follows §6.

---

## 6. Data handling

6.1 **Ownership.** Customer retains all rights in Customer Data. Vendor acquires no ownership interest in Customer Data.

6.2 **Data Processing Agreement.** Processing of Personal Data is governed by the DPA at [DPA_TEMPLATE.md](DPA_TEMPLATE.md), which is incorporated by reference. In the event of conflict between this MSA and the DPA regarding Personal Data, the DPA prevails.

6.3 **Data export.** Customer may export Customer Data during the Subscription Term using product features (DOCX, ZIP, audit CSV, API export). See the Order Form §8 for details.

6.4 **Data deletion.** After termination, Vendor deletes Customer Data within **90 days**, except where retention is required by law or documented backup cycles. Backups roll off per Vendor's retention schedule. See DPA §9 for details.

6.5 **Subprocessors.** Vendor's current subprocessor list is maintained at [SUBPROCESSORS.md](SUBPROCESSORS.md). Vendor provides **30 days'** advance notice of new subprocessors per the subprocessor change notification commitment.

6.6 **Cross-tenant patterns.** The optional cross-tenant pattern feature is **OFF by default** and requires explicit Customer opt-in. See DPA §10.

---

## 7. Service levels

7.1 **Service level objectives.** Vendor targets the service level objectives described in [SLA_SUMMARY.md](SLA_SUMMARY.md), including **99.9% monthly availability** (non-5xx request ratio), **≤ 0.1%** HTTP 5xx over the same window, and **tiered p95 latency** (infrastructure **< 300 ms**, synchronous API **< 800 ms**, AI-augmented **< 8 s** per [SLA_SUMMARY.md](SLA_SUMMARY.md) §2).

7.2 **SLA terms.** These are **objectives**, not contractual guarantees, unless a separate SLA addendum is attached to the Order Form. Enterprise-tier customers may negotiate contractual SLA terms with service credits per the Order Form.

7.3 **Planned maintenance.** Vendor provides **72 hours'** advance notice for scheduled maintenance that may affect availability, per [SLA_SUMMARY.md](SLA_SUMMARY.md) §3.

7.4 **Support.** Support levels are determined by the Customer's subscribed tier, as defined in [PRICING_PHILOSOPHY.md](PRICING_PHILOSOPHY.md) §3.

Vendor does not warrant the accuracy, completeness, or fitness for purpose of any AI Output.

VENDOR DISCLAIMS ALL OTHER WARRANTIES, EXPRESS OR IMPLIED, INCLUDING WARRANTIES OF MERCHANTABILITY, FITNESS FOR A PARTICULAR PURPOSE, AND NON-INFRINGEMENT, TO THE MAXIMUM EXTENT PERMITTED BY APPLICABLE LAW.

## 9. Limitation of liability

9.1 **Cap.** EXCEPT FOR OBLIGATIONS UNDER §10 (INDEMNIFICATION) AND BREACHES OF §2.3 (RESTRICTIONS) OR §6 (DATA HANDLING), NEITHER PARTY'S AGGREGATE LIABILITY UNDER THIS AGREEMENT SHALL EXCEED THE TOTAL FEES PAID OR PAYABLE BY CUSTOMER IN THE **12 MONTHS** PRECEDING THE EVENT GIVING RISE TO THE CLAIM.

9.2 **Exclusion of consequential damages.** NEITHER PARTY SHALL BE LIABLE FOR INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, OR LOSS OF PROFITS, REVENUE, DATA, OR BUSINESS OPPORTUNITY, HOWEVER CAUSED, EVEN IF ADVISED OF THE POSSIBILITY, TO THE MAXIMUM EXTENT PERMITTED BY APPLICABLE LAW.

9.3 **Exceptions.** The limitations in §9.1 and §9.2 do not apply to: (a) either party's indemnification obligations under §10; (b) Customer's breach of §2.3 (Restrictions); (c) Vendor's breach of §6 (Data Handling) to the extent caused by Vendor's gross negligence or willful misconduct; or (d) either party's liability for fraud.

## 10. Indemnification

10.1 **Vendor indemnification (IP).** Vendor shall defend, indemnify, and hold harmless Customer from third-party claims alleging that Customer's authorized use of the Services infringes a third party's intellectual property rights, provided Customer: (a) promptly notifies Vendor in writing; (b) grants Vendor sole control of the defense and settlement; and (c) provides reasonable cooperation.

10.2 **Vendor remedies.** If the Services become the subject of an infringement claim, Vendor may at its option: (a) procure the right for Customer to continue using the Services; (b) modify the Services to be non-infringing; or (c) terminate the affected Order Form and refund prepaid, unused fees.

10.3 **IP indemnification exclusions.** Vendor's obligations under §10.1 do not apply to claims arising from: (a) Customer's modification of the Services; (b) Customer's use of the Services in combination with non-Vendor products or services; (c) Customer Data; or (d) Customer's use of the Services after Vendor has recommended discontinuation.

10.4 **Customer indemnification.** Customer shall defend, indemnify, and hold harmless Vendor from third-party claims arising from: (a) Customer Data; (b) Customer's breach of §2.3 (Restrictions) or §3 (Customer Obligations); or (c) Customer's violation of applicable law in connection with its use of the Services.

## 11. Confidentiality

11.1 **Confidential information.** Each party's Confidential Information includes non-public information disclosed under this Agreement that is marked as confidential or that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential. Customer Data is Customer's Confidential Information. Vendor's pricing, roadmap, and unpublished documentation are Vendor's Confidential Information.

11.2 **Obligations.** The receiving party shall: (a) use Confidential Information only to exercise its rights or perform its obligations under this Agreement; (b) protect it with at least the same care it uses for its own confidential information (but no less than reasonable care); and (c) not disclose it to third parties except to employees, contractors, or agents who need to know and are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as restrictive as this section.

11.3 **Exclusions.** Information is not Confidential Information if it: (a) is or becomes publicly available without breach; (b) was known to the receiving party before disclosure; (c) is independently developed without reference to the disclosing party's information; or (d) is received from a third party without restriction.

11.4 **Compelled disclosure.** If legally compelled to disclose Confidential Information, the receiving party shall provide prompt notice to the disclosing party (to the extent legally permitted) and cooperate to limit the scope of disclosure.

## 12. General provisions

12.1 **Governing law.** This Agreement shall be governed by the law explicitly named in the applicable Order Form, without regard to conflict-of-laws provisions.

12.2 **Dispute resolution.** Disputes shall be resolved under the mechanism expressly named in the applicable Order Form (court venue or arbitration forum).

12.3 **Assignment.** Neither party may assign this Agreement without the other party's prior written consent, except in connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of substantially all assets, provided the assignee agrees in writing to be bound by this Agreement.

12.4 **Force majeure.** Neither party is liable for delays or failure to perform due to events beyond its reasonable control, including natural disasters, war, pandemic, government action, or widespread infrastructure outages, provided the affected party gives prompt notice and uses commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate.

12.5 **Notices.** Notices under this Agreement shall be in writing and delivered to the address specified in the Order Form. Email to designated contacts is sufficient for operational notices; legal notices require confirmed delivery.

12.6 **Severability.** If any provision of this Agreement is held unenforceable, the remaining provisions remain in full force and effect.

12.7 **Waiver.** Failure to enforce any provision does not constitute a waiver of future enforcement.

12.8 **Entire agreement.** This Agreement, including all Order Forms and incorporated documents, constitutes the entire agreement between the parties and supersedes all prior agreements, representations, and understandings.

12.9 **Order of precedence.** In the event of conflict between documents, the order of precedence is: Order Form > DPA > this MSA > SLA > Acceptable Use Policy.

12.10 **Amendments.** This Agreement may be amended only by a written instrument signed by both parties, except that Vendor may update Documentation, privacy policy, and the Acceptable Use Policy with **30 days'** notice.
